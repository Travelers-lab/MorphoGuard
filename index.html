Abstract

Robots in unstructured environments often face interaction tasks that require contacts beyond end-effectors. However, existing whole-body control methods usually rely on sparse or predefined contact structures, making them poorly suited to representing contacts over arbitrary body regions. This limitation stems from the lack of a body-wide, force-aware morphology representation that can uniformly encode where and how the robot interacts with the environment. To address this gap, we propose MorphoGuard, a morphology-based interaction control model for managing contacts across the whole body. Inspired by material-point (MP) discretization, MorphoGuard builds on a Whole-Body Spatial–Force Morphology Representation that encodes distributed spatial-motion and contact-force states across the robot body. By coupling MPs with electronic skin (e-skin), MorphoGuard integrates body-wide contact-force information into interactive motion modeling. Leveraging the expressive capacity of neural networks, MorphoGuard learns a mapping from current and target whole-body morphology states to joint-level commands. Comprehensive experimental evaluations show that MorphoGuard produces reliable joint commands, coordinates distributed body contacts, and enables robust real-robot execution under complex whole-body interaction conditions.

Motivation

Why do robots need morphology-aware whole-body interaction control?

Robots operating in cluttered and unstructured environments often encounter tasks that cannot be accomplished through end-effector interactions alone. Contacts may arise on forearms, elbows, and other body regions, requiring the robot to coordinate distributed and time-varying interactions.

Existing representations based on sparse terminal states or predefined contact structures are limited in describing such body-wide interactions. MorphoGuard addresses this challenge by representing robot morphology through distributed spatial–force states that encode both where interaction occurs and how contact forces evolve.
